Legislative Action - Bill S.208

S.208: An act relating to standards for law enforcement identification

S.208 would set clear statewide standards requiring law enforcement — including federal officers operating in Vermont — to identify themselves by name or badge number and not wear masks or disguises during public

Amendment

I believe the transparency provided by this bill is essential for public safety and trust. To further strengthen the intent of this legislation, I would like to propose an addendum requiring the clear identification of federal immigration enforcement vehicles operating within our state.

Mandatory Marking of Federal Enforcement Vehicles

Any vehicle operated by federal agencies engaged in civil immigration enforcement within the State of Vermont shall be clearly and conspicuously identified. Such identification shall include, but not be limited to, distinctive license plate designations or high-visibility markings that distinguish these vehicles from local law enforcement and civilian transport.

Rationale

  • Public Safety & accountability: Unmarked or non-distinct vehicles used for enforcement operations create public confusion and "vigilante" anxiety.
  • Prevention of Coercive Mimicry
  • Accountability in public spaces
