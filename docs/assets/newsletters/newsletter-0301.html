Tuesday, March 3 | 5:30 - 7:30 PM

Queen City Brewery | 703 Pine Street

Join us as we hear about the plans for No Kings Day, make signs, discuss legislation before the Vermont legislature, and catch up on all the latest doings. Want an ICE OUT lawn sign? We got 'em! $5 each at the meeting.


NO KINGS DAY March & Rally

Saturday, March 28  | Marches start at 11:00

Get ready to march! Get ready to rally! We're planning two marches - one from the South End and one from the Old North End. Signs, drums and joy! They will both meet up at City Hall Park at 12:30 PM for a brief rally with music and drumming. Stay tuned for more details.


Wanna help? We need marshals to assist marchers along the route. It's fun, makes the march safer, and supports our community. No experience necessary - we will train you! Email: QCVIMarshals@gmail.com


We also need photographers. You don’t have to be a professional. Let us know if you’d like to volunteer.  queencityphotovolunteers@gmail.com 


Drum Making for Kids (and Grown-Ups!)

Sunday, March 15 | 1:00–2:30 PM

Queen City Brewery | 703 Pine Street, Burlington

Join us for a great way to get ready for No Kings Day. We'll be making noise together and crafting our own drums! Special guest Michel from the Resistance Drummers will give us a short, fun drum lesson!  Bring your own bucket (smaller buckets work best for kids). We’ll supply the rest of the materials. Come ready to create, play, and have fun! 


Pottery for the ACLU

Saturday, March 27 | 5:00 - 8"00 PM

Burlington Beer Company | 180 Flynn Ave, Burlington

Our friends at Grounded Earth Vermont (257 Pine Street) are hosting a benefit for the ACLU. "In response to federal actions that threaten the safety and dignity of people in our communities, we’re bringing together Vermont’s clay community for a call to action. Through handmade work, we affirm our commitment to justice, peace, and the safety of all community members. 100% of proceeds from the sale go directly to the ACLU of Vermont, who defend the fundamental rights and civil liberties of all Vermonters through legal action and systemic change."


Legislative Update

Here are the bills we're following in the state house:

H.208 - Data privacy and online surveillance. (Not to be confused with S.208 - Law Enforcement Identification). 

Assigned to the House Committee on Commerce. No action taken yet.

H.742 - Providing legal representation in federal immigration proceedings

Assigned to the House Judiciary Committee. No action taken yet.

S.208 - No Secret Police - Law enforcement must show identification; no masks.

Passed by the Senate and referred to the House Judiciary Committee who have heard testimony.

S.209 - An act prohibiting civil arrest in sensitive locations

Passed by the Senate and referred to the House Judiciary Committee who have heard testimony.
