Stop faceless thugs from roaming our streets

Our calls and emails about S209 (No warrantless arrests in protected places) saved the bill!
It passed the House and will head back to the Senate.
In the meantime, the House Judiciary Committee stripped federal agents from S208 which requires law enforcement to be identifiable and not cover their faces.
Now is the time to reach out to your reps and tell them to put back the controls on federal agents.
No secret police in Vermont!

Call the Sergeant-at-Arms (802-828-2228) and leave a message for your rep.
They'll ask your name and town. Tell them to to add federal agents back into S208 (Law Enforcement Identification) and vote YES.
Don't know your rep? Ask the Sergeant or look here.

Lean Left Phonebanking

Every Sunday | 4:00-6:00 PM
From your computer

Yahoo!
Virginia redistricting wins! 51 to 49 percent. Kudos to our postcarders and phone bankers who helped make this happen!
This is a clear example that these efforts work in close contests. We were part of a team that made over 1,000,000 calls!
Critical midterm elections are approaching and many elections are won by tiny margins. Phone banking is one of the most effective actions we can take.

The goal is helping folks register and vote - not pushing specific candidates.
All you need is your computer. Training provided.

Weekly ICE Vigils

Every Wednesday | 4:00-5:00 PM (new time, starting 3/25)
ICE Federal Data Center | 188 Harvest Lane, Williston
Park at East Rise Credit Union

Bring a bench, chair or cushion, warm clothes, blanket, hand and feet warmers, umbrella. Our meditative sit is an opportunity to work with the challenging energy of this time energy which manifests in many ways, including the suffering caused by ICE in our community, in our world, and in our hearts. Our aspiration is not to judge the people who work in the building, but to change the energy that leads to present attitudes and policies.

If you choose to bring a sign, we hope that your message will respectfully raise questions about ICE's policies and actions, and convey our appreciation for migrants, and their right to fair and legal treatment. More info: Anne Damrosch.
